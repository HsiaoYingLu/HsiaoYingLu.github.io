About Me

I am a Computer Science PhD student in University of California, Davis, advised by Prof. Kwan-Liu Ma, focusing on Network Data Analysis using machine learning in combination with visual analytics. I have been working on Explainable Graph Neural Networks and extracting associations among network attributes and structural characteristics. One of the target application is to improve the healthcare cooperation efficiency and enhance the quality of treatments given to cancer patients. Before, I was a research assistant in Academia Sinica, advised by Drs. Jen-Chun Lin and Hong-Yuan Mark Liao, developing Computational Creativity with a sequential multimodal generative model. I received my two B.S degree in Computer Science and Information Engineering & Electrical Engineering at National Central University, Taiwan. My research interests include Network Data Analysis, Machine Learning, Explainable AI, and Visual Analytics. If you are interested in working with me or would like to know more about me, please contact me via hyllu@ucdavis.edu.
