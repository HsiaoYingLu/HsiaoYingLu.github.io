Road Segmentation and Object Detection Models for Autonomous Driving System

These models are aim to assist autonomous driving system to "see" the road/lane markings and objects, such like pedestrians. Also, it is designed to "read" traffic signal/sign.

I used YOLOv3 Darknet version as the backbone of the object detection model and ENet as the segmentation model. While studying and fine-tuning the architectures, I ported them to the Nvidia Drive Pegasus by converting the models into TensorRT ones with reduced floating point precision from FP32 to FP16 and INT8. The models with FP16 precision could still output results with comparable accuracy, and even reduce the precision to INT8 without a reduction in performance for the segmentation model. Since layers like PReLU and customized upsampling layer are not originally supported by TensorRT, I wrote my own plugin layers in cuda code.

The part I developed was then ported onto a real vehicle, enabling it to automatically stop when “seeing” pedestrians walk by or a traffic light turn red.

Road/Lane Segmentation result tested on KITTI Road/Lane Detection Evaluation 2013 Benchmark
